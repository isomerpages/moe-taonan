<p><strong>2026年校长献词&nbsp;&nbsp;</strong>
<br><strong><u>道南学校建校120周年庆</u></strong>
</p>
<p>1906 年11 月18 日，桥北路的一所大宅内传出朗朗的读书声。为了解决早期移民子女的教育问题，由福建社团创办的学府——道南学堂，在百名福建商人的支持下，于岛国扎根。“道南”二字，意思是“将儒家的道德思想弘扬于南洋”。1916年，福建先贤高瞻远瞩，将位于亚美尼亚街的道南学校改以华语取代福建方言，成为本地第一所以华语为教学媒介的新型华校。学校经历了二战的洗礼，也见证了我国独立后的教育改革与发展。从80年代初由市区迁至马林弯现址以来，道南学校在新加坡福建会馆的大力支持下稳健发展，为国家灌溉幼苗，培育社会栋梁。</p>
<p>2026年，学校将迎来建校120周年的重要里程碑。为纪念这一历史性的时刻，学校在去年举办了建校120周年徽标及主题征集活动，在此感谢师生、校友及家长的踊跃参与。&nbsp;</p>
<p>建校120周年校庆主题“道承双甲，南泽八方”，由小五学生蔡源旖所拟，蕴含着深厚的历史底蕴与面向未来的使命担当。“道承”体现了学校自创校以来，始终秉持“诚毅”校训，代代相传的精神与价值；“双甲”则象征学校历经百廿光阴的辉煌与坚韧；“南泽八方”寓意道南所传递的教育力量，像水一样滋养、惠泽社会各个角落，培育无数栋梁之才。主题既是对过去的致敬，更是对未来的期许，彰显学校持续传承与创新的教育理念。在百廿新起点上，道南将携手师生、校友及家长，共谱新章，续写辉煌。</p>
<p>建校 120 周年徽标，出自2024届毕业校友白颖暄之手，以视觉设计精准地诠释了校庆主题。以“120”为主体构图，其中的“0”设计为无穷符号（∞），象征道南将继续学无止境与茁壮发展。三条交织的线条代表学校的过去、现在与未来，体现出道南在传承与创新中一脉相承的精神。整体主色调为学校代表色蓝，沉稳而富希望；下方展开的书本象征知识与学习的根基；红色印章篆刻“诚毅”，点明道南百年来不变的价值核心。</p>
<p>承载深刻意义的校庆主题与徽标，象征着道南人的共同记忆与愿景。我们诚挚邀请师生、校友及家长一同参与这一值得铭刻的建校120周年庆典。庆祝活动的详情会在日后公布，敬请期待！</p>
<p>校长
<br>傅勤予女士</p>

<p><strong>2026 Principal’s Message&nbsp;</strong>
<br><strong>Tao Nan School Celebrating 120 Years</strong>
</p>
<p>In 2026, Tao Nan School will mark its 120th anniversary. This celebration
is a tribute to 120 years of dedication to education, a journey that began
with a visionary purpose. In the early days, our founders, the Hokkien
Clan Association and supporting Hokkien merchants, were committed to nurturing
the children of early migrants. Their dedication led to the opening of <em>Daonan Xuetang</em> on
18 November 1906 at North Bridge Road. Our pioneering spirit was evident
early on. In 1916, following the move to Armenian Street, the school made
history by switching the medium of instruction from Hokkien to Mandarin
– becoming the first school in Singapore to do so. Through the decades, <em>Daonan</em> endured
the challenges of World War II and navigated post-independence education
reforms. Since its relocation to Marine Crescent in the early ‘80s, Tao
Nan School, steadfastly supported by the Singapore Hokkien Huay Kuan, has
progressed steadily, committed to educating young minds to become pillars
of our society.</p>
<p>To commemorate this historic milestone, we are proud to unveil our official <em>120th Anniversary Theme and Logo</em>,
conceived with the valued input of our students, staff, alumni and parents,
following our call for submissions last year.</p>
<p>The <em>120<sup>th</sup> Anniversary Theme</em> “<em>Celebrating Legacy, Inspiring Horizons</em>”
was created by our Primary 5 student Chua Yuen Yi. The theme eloquently
captures the dual focus of our celebration: <em>Celebrating Legacy</em> signifies
our proud 120-year history, honouring the foundational values of Sincerity
and Perseverance passed down through generations. <em>Inspiring Horizons</em> embodies
our commitment to nurturing students who will make meaningful contributions
to society, ensuring Tao Nan's positive influence on education continues
far and wide. This theme pays tribute to a remarkable past while firmly
looking ahead to the future, uniting our community as we enter a new, inspiring
chapter.</p>
<p>The <em>120th Anniversary Logo, </em>designed by Peh Ying Xuan, Ulyss,
a graduate from the Class of 2024, visually encapsulates the spirit of
the theme. The prominent numeral “120” features a stylized infinity symbol
(∞), representing the school’s continuous pursuit of knowledge and boundless
growth. This dynamic form is enhanced by three interwoven lines that symbolise
Tao Nan’s past, present, and future, visually reinforcing our enduring
legacy and forward-looking vision. The blue palette signifies wisdom and
hope, while the open book at the base reflects the fundamental importance
of learning. Finally, the red seal bearing the school’s motto, “Sincerity
and Perseverance,” reinforces the values at the heart of our institution.</p>
<p>Inspired by the meaningful theme and logo, students, staff, alumni and
parents are warmly invited to celebrate this commemorative event with us.
We look forward to sharing further details on the exciting events and programmes
planned throughout 2026.</p>
<p>Principal
<br>Poh Qinyu</p>
